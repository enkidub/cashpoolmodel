How it works

From data to board decision
in three steps.

01
Enter your group structure
Input each entity, its average daily balance, number of accounts, and your current bank rates. Use presets for CZK, EUR, or USD from live central bank rates.
02
Run the simulation
The engine models three scenarios — base, optimistic, pessimistic — with daily cash flow volatility, netting efficiency, and full before/after P&L comparison.
03
Download the PDF report
Export a structured report ready for your CFO presentation, board paper, or bank RFP. Includes executive summary, sensitivity analysis, and implementation roadmap.

About

Treasury expertise,
without the agency markup.

I'm Jakub Houser, a treasury professional with 10+ years across banking, consulting, and corporate treasury. I've spent most of my career helping companies structure, implement, and optimise cash pooling — first as a consultant at Accenture, then as a product manager at Erste Group, a solution sales manager at Citi covering CEE, and most recently as a Senior Transaction Services Account Manager at ING in Geneva.

My master's thesis at Masaryk University was on cash pooling — specifically the commercial bank and corporate perspectives. That research, combined with hundreds of client conversations and real pool implementations, is what powers this simulator.

I built PoolSimulator because I kept seeing the same problem: treasury teams were being sold pooling solutions they didn't fully understand, at rates they couldn't validate, with business cases built on vendor spreadsheets. This tool gives CFOs and treasury managers an independent way to model the decision before committing.

Beyond the simulator, I offer independent treasury advisory — particularly for groups expanding into CEE, evaluating TMS vendors, or structuring intercompany financing for the first time.
